Historical places

Petit Trianon

The Petit Trianon, considered to be royal architect Ange-Jacques Gabriel's masterpiece, is something of a manifesto for the neo-classical movement

Mount Fuji

Mt. Fuji is the tallest peak in Japan, the result of volcanic activity that began approximately 100,000 years ago.

Royal Palace

Louis XIII's old hunting pavilion was transformed and extended by his son, Louis XIV, when he installed the Court and government there in 1682.

Kellies Castle

Kellie's Castle is situated beside the Raya River (Sungai Raya), which is a small creek to the Kinta River.
